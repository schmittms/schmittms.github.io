About Me

I am a physics PhD student at the University of Chicago working with Prof. Vincenzo Vitelli on projects at the intersection of physics, machine learning, and biology.

During my PhD I have worked on several projects: predicting and modeling traction force generation in single cells; theoretically studying the optimal compression of dynamical systems; and identifying functional groups of species in complex microbial communities directly from data.

Although these projects may appear unrelated at first glance (and at second glance), they are united by the overarching goal of using machine learning to identify interpretable low-dimensional models directly from complex, high-dimensional data in settings where standard physics approaches fail.
